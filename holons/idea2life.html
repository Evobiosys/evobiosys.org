What is idea2.life?

idea2.life is a Life OS — a sovereign infrastructure layer for people and organizations who refuse to rent their digital existence from corporations. It is the EvoBioSys holon that asks: what does it take to own your communications, your data, your DNS, and your hosting — without becoming a full-time systems administrator?

The project spans self-hosted communication (Matrix, Signal bridges), private DNS and hosting infrastructure, and the connective tissue that ties these services into a coherent, maintainable whole. idea2.life is not a single product but a constellation of sovereign services that together form a complete digital operating system.

idea2.life is tagged #my-legacy — a long-term commitment to sovereign infrastructure that outlasts any individual platform or provider. It has a dedicated workspace in Tana, a published OnePager, and an active open tech stack philosophy: where possible, every component is open-source, self-hostable, and replaceable.

Signal Orbit / Signal Hub

Signal Hub is the communication nerve center of idea2.life. It manages the flow of signals — messages, notifications, updates — across the sovereign infrastructure, ensuring that communication stays private, encrypted, and under your control.

The project bridges multiple messaging protocols into a unified, self-hosted layer. Rather than scattering conversations across proprietary platforms, Signal Hub consolidates them into a sovereign communication fabric that you own and operate.

Capabilities

  • Encrypted messaging across federated protocols
  • Signal bridge integration for mainstream contacts
  • Notification and signal flow management
  • Self-hosted, no third-party dependencies

Jakaja-Matrix

Jakaja-Matrix is the self-hosted Matrix server at the heart of idea2.life's communication layer. Matrix is a federated, encrypted communication protocol — the open alternative to Slack, Discord, and Teams — and Jakaja-Matrix is its sovereign instantiation within the EvoBioSys network.

Server details

  • Valid Element.io server — fully compatible with the Element client ecosystem, accessible from any Matrix-compatible app.
  • Federation enabled — Jakaja-Matrix federates with other Matrix homeservers, allowing communication across the decentralized Matrix network without compromising sovereignty.
  • Signal bridge — a bridge that connects the Matrix server to the Signal protocol, enabling encrypted messaging with Signal contacts directly from the Matrix interface.
  • systemd service — runs as a managed systemd service for reliability, automatic restarts, and clean integration with the host operating system.

Jakaja-Matrix serves as both a production communication server and a proving ground for idea2.life's self-hosting philosophy. It demonstrates that sovereign, federated communication is not only possible but practical — with the right tooling and operational discipline.

Hosting & DNS

Sovereign infrastructure starts at the foundation: who controls your DNS, and where do your services run? idea2.life addresses this through its best hosting sub-holon — a curated approach to private DNS and hosting infrastructure.

Principles

  • Private DNS. Domain resolution should not be a surveillance vector. idea2.life uses privacy-respecting DNS configurations that prevent third parties from logging your browsing and service discovery patterns.
  • EU-sovereign hosting. Services run on infrastructure within EU jurisdiction, subject to EU data protection law rather than US CLOUD Act reach.
  • Self-hostable by design. Every service in the idea2.life stack can be migrated to new infrastructure. There is no vendor lock-in at the hosting level.
  • Open tech stack. The full infrastructure configuration is documented and, where possible, published — so that others can replicate the sovereign setup for their own needs.

idea2.life connects to EvoBioSys's broader Publication Pipeline for documenting and sharing its infrastructure patterns, and to the kidur holon for the open-source tooling philosophy that underpins every technical decision.
