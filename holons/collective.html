What is the Collective?

The EvoBioSys Collective is the governance and coordination structure that holds the holonic network together. It is not a board of directors or a management layer — it is a living collective that stewards the shared practices, principles, and agreements by which all holons operate.

Founded on March 9, 2026 — alongside the broader founding of EvoBioSys's formal structure — the Collective exists because a network of autonomous holons needs a coordination mechanism that is itself holonic: whole in its own right, yet embedded in the larger system it serves.

The Collective does not command. It coordinates, convenes, and holds space for decisions that affect the whole network. Its authority comes from the consent of its members and the coherence of the principles it upholds.

Structure

The Collective operates as a holarchy — nested circles of governance, each with its own scope and autonomy:

  • Super-holon: EvoBioSys. The Collective is a holon within EvoBioSys, responsible for the governance dimension of the whole network. It does not sit above the other holons; it sits alongside them, serving a specific function.
  • Sub-holon: SoFin Collective. The first nested collective, responsible for the social finance domain. The SoFin Collective demonstrates the pattern: as domains grow in complexity, they can spin off their own governance bodies while remaining connected to the whole.

This structure is designed to scale. As EvoBioSys grows, new domain-specific collectives can form — each with its own membership, practices, and decision-making processes — while the EvoBioSys Collective maintains overall coherence and shared agreements.

Bio

SoFin Collective

The SoFin Collective governs the social finance domain within EvoBioSys. It stewards the Funding Flow Machine, evaluates crypto and trading initiatives, and manages the financial tools and protocols that serve the broader network.

As a nested holon, it operates with its own membership and practices while remaining aligned with the EvoBioSys Collective's shared principles. It demonstrates that governance can be distributed without being fragmented.

Membership

The Collective currently operates with a founding membership that reflects its early stage. As the network grows, membership will expand to include stewards from across the holonic map.

Current members

  • Jakob Possert-Bienzle — founding member. Steward of EvoBioSys, lead of multiple holons across all three pillars. Holds the founding vision and coordinates the Collective's initial practices and agreements.

Membership principles

  • Stewardship, not ownership. Members are stewards of the shared commons, not owners of assets. The Collective holds resources in trust for the network.
  • Consent-based decisions. Major decisions affecting the whole network require consent from Collective members — not unanimous agreement, but the absence of principled objection.
  • Active participation. Membership is not honorary. Members contribute to governance, attend convening sessions, and engage with the decisions that shape the network.
  • Open to growth. The Collective is designed to expand. As holons mature and new stewards emerge, membership grows to reflect the increasing complexity and distributed wisdom of the network.
