What is Soaro?

Soaro is the consulting and stewardship holon within the Bio pillar of EvoBioSys. It works with donor-recipient organisations and funders who understand that isolated efforts fail where coordinated efforts thrive.

The central challenge Soaro addresses is effectively allocating resources across complex, interdependent projects. Most organisations face this challenge as a combinatorial puzzle — too many moving parts, too little visibility, too slow a feedback loop. Soaro treats it as a solvable system problem: a shared-outcome algorithm that maps interdependencies and reveals the moves that unlock the most value for everyone involved.

Think of it as solving a Rubik’s cube: each face represents a stakeholder, each colour a resource or need. Random turns get nowhere. An algorithm — informed by metadata, real-time feedback, and the actual structure of the problem — solves it efficiently. That is what Soaro builds.

The Soaro Five Framework

Every Soaro engagement is structured around five pillars that together form the standard offer. They move a collaboration from abstract goodwill to concrete, measurable shared success.

  1. Framework — The Soaro Five itself: a reusable coordination architecture that maps stakeholders, needs, and interdependencies into a legible structure.
  2. Funding Model — A transparent approach to pooling, directing, and accounting for financial resources across partner organisations.
  3. Platform for Collaboration — A digital workspace (currently moving toward MVP) where partners coordinate in real time, share data, and track progress together.
  4. Bite-Sized Prompting — Micro-interventions that keep teams aligned without heavy process overhead — short prompts, quick check-ins, focused nudges.
  5. Shared Success Pathways — Defined outcomes that all partners commit to, with clear metrics and a feedback loop that makes progress visible to everyone.

Platform Modules

What the Soaro workspace does

The Soaro Stewardship workspace — currently in development with Alia Tall-Khatib — is composed of interconnected modules. Together they form a metadata-powered coordination layer for multi-stakeholder initiatives.

Research

Structured research capability that surfaces relevant data, precedent, and evidence to inform decisions across all partners.

Personalised Journey

Adaptive pathways that meet each participant where they are, guiding them through onboarding, learning, and contribution at their own pace.

Local Partners

Working with on-the-ground organisations to anchor global coordination in local knowledge, relationships, and trust.

Interdependent Opportunities

Illuminating the hidden connections between projects — where one organisation’s output is another’s input, and collaboration becomes a multiplier.

Needs Identification

Systematic surfacing of unmet needs across the network, turning gaps into actionable items that partners can collectively address.

Metadata-Powered Synergy

Structured data about activities, outputs, and intentions enables algorithmic matching — the engine behind the shared-outcome algorithm.

Group Chat & Connections

Integrated communication and facilitated introductions that reduce friction between people who should be working together.

Tactical Support

Hands-on operational help — from project management and capacity building to knowledge management and resource libraries.

Measurement & Evaluation

Real-time feedback loops and structured M&E so that every partner can see what is working, what is not, and what to adjust next.
