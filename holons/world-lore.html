What is World Lore?

World Lore is the EvoBioSys holon dedicated to historical knowledge and cultural memory. It exists because the stories we tell about the past shape the futures we consider possible — and too many of those stories are incomplete, distorted, or deliberately silenced.

The project focuses on recovering, preserving, and transmitting deep historical knowledge — particularly the threads that standard historiography has undervalued or overlooked. This is not antiquarianism. It is the conviction that a civilization that has lost contact with its roots cannot orient itself in the present.

World Lore sits in the Evo pillar (growing), alongside EvoPaideia and Community Hubs, because historical understanding is a form of cultural development. To know where you come from is to expand the field of what you can become.

Sub-holon

European History

A book project recovering the European non-Roman indigenous past. Currently at v0.1 draft.

The European History project — formerly titled "Celtic History" — is World Lore's flagship initiative. It is a book-length work that recovers the indigenous civilizations of Europe before and beyond Roman dominion.

Standard European history begins with Greece and Rome, treating everything before and beside them as "barbarian" background. This book challenges that framing by tracing the deep population strands that actually shaped the continent:

  • Western Hunter-Gatherers — the oldest continuous populations of Europe, whose genetic and cultural legacy persists in ways that are only now being understood through archaeogenetics.
  • Early European Farmers — the Neolithic wave from Anatolia and the Levant that brought agriculture, settled life, and new social structures to a continent of foragers.
  • Steppe Herders — the Yamnaya and related populations who swept westward from the Pontic Steppe in the Bronze Age, carrying Indo-European languages, horse culture, and a pastoral way of life.

The book weaves these strands together with archaeological evidence, linguistics, mythology, and contemporary archaeogenetic research. It pays particular attention to figures and events at the edges of the Roman encounter — including original research on Brennus and the Gallic sack of Rome, a pivotal episode that reveals the sophistication and strategic capability of non-Roman European societies.

The project has its own Matrix space for collaboration and a dedicated VPS folder for research materials. The current draft (v0.1) is undergoing expansion and peer review.

Sub-holon

Historical Digital Maps

Interactive maps that make historical territories, migrations, and cultural boundaries visible and explorable.

Historical Digital Maps is the technical counterpart to the European History book project. Where the book offers narrative and analysis, the maps offer spatial understanding — the ability to see where peoples lived, how they moved, and how territories shifted over centuries and millennia.

The project aims to build interactive, web-based maps that allow users to explore:

  • Migration routes of hunter-gatherer, farmer, and steppe populations
  • Archaeological site distributions and their cultural associations
  • Linguistic boundaries and their evolution over time
  • Trade networks and exchange routes across pre-Roman Europe

By combining geographic data with the research from the European History project, Historical Digital Maps transforms static historical knowledge into a navigable, spatial experience — making the deep past tangible in a way that text alone cannot.

Connected publications

World Lore's research feeds into and draws from several connected projects across the EvoBioSys network:

  • Aligned European Ideology — a writing project that builds on World Lore's historical research to articulate a contemporary European identity rooted in indigenous heritage rather than imperial nostalgia. The European History sub-holon provides the evidential foundation for this work.
  • EvoPaideia — historical understanding as a dimension of developmental education. World Lore provides the cultural memory that EvoPaideia integrates into its learning frameworks.
  • Publication Pipeline — both the European History book and the Historical Digital Maps are part of EvoBioSys's broader publication strategy, moving from internal drafts through peer review to public release.
