Publications

Writing as a living practice. Our publication projects are holons in their own right — evolving drafts published early and built in the open, not polished artifacts delivered once and sealed.

Approach

Publishing as practice

EvoBioSys treats writing the same way it treats software: publish early, iterate often, build in the open. We do not wait until something is perfect to share it. A draft is a living thing — it grows through encounter with readers, through friction with reality, through the slow accumulation of clarity.

Each publication project is a project holon — autonomous in its own direction, nested within the broader Evo pillar. Some are books in progress, others are frameworks taking shape. All are invitations to think along.

Our writing draws on the World Lore holon for historical research, on EvoPaideia for developmental and educational framing, and on the lived experience of building sovereign, living systems in real time. The sources are diverse; the purpose is singular: to articulate the ideas that the transition to a new world requires.

Aligned European Ideology

A book project articulating an aligned European ideology — one that draws on indigenous roots, integral philosophy, and a post-colonial reckoning with European history. This is not nationalism and not globalism; it is an attempt to find the developmental core of European identity and build forward from there.

Currently at v0.1 draft. The work integrates historical research from the European History sub-holon with developmental frameworks from EvoPaideia and the practical sovereignty questions that EvoBioSys confronts daily.

  • EvoPaideia — Developmental and integral framing

European History: The Non-Roman Indigenous Past

A book project recovering the pre-Roman, indigenous history of Europe — the deep past that dominant narratives have compressed or erased. Formerly titled "Celtic History," the scope has expanded to encompass the full complexity of Europe's ancestral landscape.

Three big strands weave through the work: the Western hunter-gatherers who shaped the continent's earliest cultures, the Early European Farmers who brought agriculture and settlement from the Near East, and the steppe herders whose migrations reshaped the genetic and cultural map of Europe. Together, these strands reveal a past far richer and more diverse than the classical narrative allows.

Connected holons

  • World Lore — Parent research umbrella
  • Aligned European Ideology — Ideological framework drawing on this research
  • Historical Digital Maps — Visual and cartographic companion
Evo Vision

Universal Policy

A writing project exploring universal policy — principles and frameworks that could guide governance across cultural and national boundaries while honoring developmental complexity. Where "Aligned European Ideology" looks inward at European identity, "Universal Policy" looks outward at the structures all societies need.

The project asks: what policies would a civilization design if it started from living systems principles? What does governance look like when sovereignty is mutual and holarchic rather than competitive and hierarchical?

Connected holons

  • Aligned European Ideology — Regional application of universal principles
  • EvoPaideia — Developmental stage awareness in policy design

Research Foundation

World Lore

All publication projects draw on World Lore — the historical knowledge umbrella within the Evo pillar. World Lore is not a publication itself but the research substrate from which publications grow. It holds the accumulated historical knowledge, source material, and analytical frameworks that inform the writing.

European History, Historical Digital Maps, and the broader cultural memory work all live under World Lore. When a publication project needs historical grounding, it reaches into World Lore; when research surfaces something that deserves its own articulation, a new publication holon may emerge.

Looking Ahead

The publication pipeline

What is growing beneath the surface — and the systems we use to cultivate it.

Publication Pipeline

A structured workflow for moving ideas from raw signal to published artifact. The pipeline tracks the lifecycle of each piece — from initial capture in the Peak Signal Pool, through drafting and review, to publication. It ensures nothing promising gets lost and nothing premature gets forced.

Puzzle Piece Garden

Not every insight is ready to be a publication. The Puzzle Piece Garden is where fragments live — ideas, quotes, half-formed arguments, striking data points. They accumulate, cross-pollinate, and eventually find their place in a larger work. The garden metaphor is deliberate: you plant, you water, you wait.

Peak Signal Pool

The intake layer. High-signal sources, prompts, and provocations that feed the publication pipeline. When something resonates — a paper, a conversation, a historical finding — it enters the pool and gets routed to the appropriate project holon.

Onboarding Documents

Currently at v0.0.2. Structured onboarding materials for new collaborators entering the EvoBioSys ecosystem — explaining the holonic structure, the publication approach, and how to contribute to active writing projects.
